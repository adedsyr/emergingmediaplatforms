About Me

My name is Austin De Dios. I hail from a town south of Salt Lake City, Utah, called Sandy. After graduating from the University of Portland with a bachelor's degree in economics, I moved on to The Oregonian as an Advance Local Media fellow. I cover diversity, equity and inclusion, as well as public safety. In my spare time, I enjoy reading, writing short horror stories and exploring Oregon's plentiful forests.
